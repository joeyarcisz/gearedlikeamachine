**Post:**
The title "Director of Photography" undersells the job.

A DP commands three departments simultaneously: camera, lighting, and grip. They design every visual element before call time, manage the lighting scheme that takes 60% of the shoot day, and make the real-time decisions that determine whether footage looks like cinema or a webcam recording.

I wrote a breakdown of the full scope of the role, including what happens in pre-production, what the decision anatomy of a single setup looks like, and how the DP rate actually breaks down as a percentage of a total production day.
