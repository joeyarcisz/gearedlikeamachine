Normal lenses disappear and let the performance carry the scene. There is a reason Lubezki shot close-ups on a 12mm in The Revenant, and a reason Deakins reaches for wide primes on faces. ## LinkedIn Post

There is a question every DP should be asking before the camera package arrives on location: what does this character feel in this moment, and which focal length puts the audience inside that feeling?

Wide lenses distort at close range. Characters cannot hide. Telephoto lenses compress the world behind the subject until there is no escape. Normal lenses disappear entirely, which is the hardest thing to pull off well.
