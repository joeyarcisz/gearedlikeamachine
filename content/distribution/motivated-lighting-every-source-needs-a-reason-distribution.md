## LinkedIn Post

One question separates disciplined lighting from lighting that just happened to be there: why does this source exist in this scene?

Motivated lighting is not a stylistic preference. It is the foundational logic that makes audiences stop noticing the lighting at all. When every source traces back to a practical in the scene's world, the illusion holds. When one source cannot be explained, the audience feels it, even if they cannot name it.

New post on the Geared Like A Machine blog breaks down the complete system: the three categories of motivation (direct practical, implied source, atmospheric), how two motivated sources at different color temperatures create earned tension rather than confusion, and the on-set test to run before every setup is called ready.
