Pre-production is not overhead. It is the work.

The most visually stunning films ever made were meticulously planned. Roger Deakins walked every location on No Country for Old Men months before a camera arrived. Emmanuel Lubezki rehearsed the Bexhill sequence from Children of Men until the 7-minute single take lived in both his and Cuaron's bodies before it lived on film.

The new GLM post breaks down the five tools that determine whether a shoot day goes well or falls apart: shot list, tech scout, storyboard, blocking diagram, and script breakdown. It comes with a fully interactive companion, including a working tech scout checklist and a quiz that tests how you read a scene description as a DP.

For anyone who hires DPs, produces shoots, or is building their own pre-production discipline, this is a useful read.
