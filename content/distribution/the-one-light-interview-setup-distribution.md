## LinkedIn Post

Most interview lighting exists to fill the frame. The one-light setup exists to say something about the subject.

One source, positioned at roughly 45 degrees and slightly above eye level. One black V-flat on the shadow side. The subject pulled at least six feet from the background so inverse-square law does the separation work. These are not three-point lighting minus two lights. It is a system with its own logic.

New article on the GLM site breaks down the full framework: key quality and what it does to shadow edge, why negative fill is a contrast decision not a hardware decision, and the catch light test that determines whether a setup is finished.

There is also an interactive tool that lets you adjust each variable and see the result in real time.
