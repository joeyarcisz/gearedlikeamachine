## LinkedIn Post

Producers hire DPs based on reels and day rates. Both are unreliable signals if you don't know what to look for.

A reel is a curated artifact: best frames, best projects, often sourced from multiple operators under the same credit, always cut to music that does half the emotional lifting. Turn the sound off. See what changes.

The day rate is noise without context. A $3,500/day DP bringing a gaffer and camera operator is running a five-person team through a pre-designed shoot day. A $900 solo shooter is doing something categorically different. Neither number means anything without knowing what it includes.

I wrote a guide on what to actually evaluate: four interview questions that separate working DPs from talented shooters, how to structure day rate vs. project rate for commercial work, and a red flag checklist built from what goes wrong in real productions.
