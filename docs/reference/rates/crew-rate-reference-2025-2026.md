# GLM Crew Rate Reference — 2025-2026
## Comprehensive Freelance Day Rates Across All Departments
### Market: Texas / DFW (Non-Union) + National Benchmarks

---

**Compiled:** March 2026
**Sources:** IATSE wage schedules (Locals 600, 695, 706, 728, 798, 800, 884), DGA rate cards (2025-2026), SAG-AFTRA theatrical/commercial contracts, Teamsters Local 399 agreements, AICP commercial production agreement (2025-2028), Freelance Video Collective, Wrapbook, Assemble, Mixing Light, School of Motion, AIR 2025 Rate Guide, "Know Your Worth" peer survey, DTX Media (Dallas), PayScale, Glassdoor, ZipRecruiter, Texas Film Commission, and 40+ additional industry sources.

**Key Context:**
- All rates are **10-hour day** unless noted otherwise
- Texas is **right-to-work** — most DFW productions are non-union
- DFW rates run **15-25% below LA/NYC** for equivalent roles
- **IATSE Local 484** (Dallas) covers Texas; rate cards are member-restricted
- Texas **SB22 film incentive** (effective Sept 1, 2025): up to 31% cash rebate on qualified TX spending
- Kit fees, equipment rentals, and materials are **always separate from labor** per Joey's Rate Integrity Rules

## 20. RATE MODIFIERS & BILLING STRUCTURE

### Production Type Multiplier

| Type | Rate vs. Mid-Market | Budget Tier | Day Standard |
|------|-------------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Corporate / Branded** | 60–75% of mid | Medium | 8–10 hrs |
| **Commercial (Regional)** | 90–100% of mid | Medium-High | 10–12 hrs |
| **Commercial (National/Agency)** | 100–130% of mid | Highest | 10–12 hrs |
| **Narrative Indie** | 80–100% of mid | Low-Medium | 12 hrs |
| **Narrative (Studio/Streaming)** | 120–180% of mid | High | 12 hrs |

### Overtime Structure (Non-Union Standard)
- Hours 1–8: 1x (straight time)
- Hours 9–12: 1.5x
- Hours 13–16: 2x
- Hour 17+: **Golden Time** (each hour = full day rate)
- Turnaround violation (under 10 hrs): Start next day at 1.5x

### Weekly Rate Discount
- Industry standard: **weekly = 4x daily** (work 5 days, pay for 4)
- DGA weekly rates confirm this ratio ($7,364/wk ÷ $1,841/day = 4.0x)

### Production Company Markup When Billing Clients

| Component | Percentage |
|-----------|-----------|
| Production company overhead | 15–25% of total budget |
| Production fee (profit margin) | 20–50% (higher % on smaller budgets) |
| Payroll burden / fringes (union) | 24–34% on top of crew wages |

### Texas SB22 Film Incentive (Effective Sept 1, 2025)
- **Up to 31% cash rebate** on qualified in-state spending
- Base rate: 25% + up to 6% in stackable uplifts
- TX resident crew wages count toward qualifying spend
- Effective crew cost after rebate: $1,500 crew day → ~$1,035 net
- TX resident requirement: 35% of crew (2025-2027), rising to 50% by 2031
- $1.5 billion total over 10 years ($300M per biennium)
