# 1. PRICING MODELS OVERVIEW

## Project-Based Flat Fee (Most Common)

The dominant model for established production companies. Client gets a single all-in price after scoping.

| Project Type | Budget | Mid-Market | Premium |
|---|---|---|---|
| Talking head / testimonial (1-2 min) | $2,500-$5,000 | $7,500-$15,000 | $15,000-$30,000 |
| Brand / product video | $5,000-$12,000 | $15,000-$35,000 | $35,000-$100,000+ |
| TV commercial (regional) | $5,000-$12,000 | $15,000-$35,000 | $35,000-$100,000 |
| TV commercial (national) | N/A | N/A | $100,000-$500,000+ |
| Full campaign (multi-spot) | $25,000-$60,000 | $50,000-$150,000 | $150,000-$500,000+ |

## Day Rate Model

Used when providing "camera crew for hire" rather than full-service production.

- Standard shoot day: **10 hours** (some define as 8 + 2-hour buffer before OT)
- Half day: anything under 4.5 hours
- Half day rate: **60-75% of full day** (not 50%, because crew holds the time)

## Hourly Rate

Primarily used for post-production and scope overages, not full productions.

- US-based agencies: **$100-$175/hour** for key personnel
- High end: **$200-$350/hour**
- Editing specifically: **$75-$200/hour**

## Value-Based Pricing

Price based on deliverable value to client, not your costs. A $30,000 brand video that helps close $500K in B2B sales is worth more than the $18,000 it costs to produce.

**When to use:** Direct-to-brand work, concept-driven projects, clients who don't care about line items, projects with clear ROI.

**When NOT to use:** AICP/agency bids, clients who retain audit rights, competitive bids where transparency is required.

## Hybrid Models (Most Practical)

- **Day rate for production + flat fee for post**: Transparency on variable shoot costs, locked-in post scope
- **Base package + a la carte add-ons**: Core deliverable at set price, menu pricing for extras
- **Phased billing**: Invoiced in three phases aligned to pre-production, shoot, and delivery

# 2. MARKUP STRUCTURES & PROFIT MATH

## Crew Markup

Two layers when billing freelance crew to clients:

### Layer 1: Payroll Burden (Hard Cost Recovery)
If running crew through payroll (W-2 via Wrapbook, Media Services, etc.):

| Item | Rate |
|---|---|
| FICA (SS + Medicare employer) | 7.65% |
| FUTA | ~0.6% |
| Texas SUTA | ~2.7% |
| Workers' Comp (TX production) | 1.5-3% |
| **Total payroll burden** | **12-15%** |
| Union P&W (if applicable) | adds 24-34% on top |

### Layer 2: Company Overhead Markup (Profit Layer)

| Context | Markup |
|---|---|
| Lean/budget work minimum | 15-20% |
| Standard commercial production | 22-28% |
| AICP-style standard | 25% |
| Rate card / full price | 25-30% |

### Real-World Example

| Line Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Freelance DP day rate | $1,200 |
| Payroll burden (13%) | $156 |
| Hard cost to company | $1,356 |
| 25% markup | $339 |
| **Billed to client** | **$1,695** |

Simplified method: many companies just markup the freelancer's rate by **30-40%** and call it done.

## Equipment Markup

### Gear You Own (Biggest Profit Lever for a Small Company)

Charge clients at or near **market rental house rates** regardless of what you paid.

**The 3-5% Rule:** Charge 3-5% of gear's purchase price per day as rental fee.

| Item | Purchase Price | 3%/day | 5%/day | Rental House Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| RED V-Raptor | $14,500 | $435 | $725 | $500-$750 |
| ARRI Signature Prime set | $125,000 | $3,750 | $6,250 | $2,500-$4,000 |

**Payoff math:** RED V-Raptor at $500/day pays off in 29 shooting days. After that, every rental day is nearly pure profit.

### Sub-Rental Markup (Gear You Rent From a House)

| Context | Markup |
|---|---|
| AICP maximum | 20% |
| Standard | 15% |
| Default for established clients | 15% |
| New/unknown clients | 20% |

### Kit Fees

- Small personal kit: **$150-$400/day**
- Full camera package (body + lenses + media + accessories): **$500-$900/day**

## Post-Production Markup

### In-House Editor

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Employee editor salary | $55K-$75K/year = $26-$36/hr true cost |
| Fully loaded cost (benefits, taxes, equipment, software) | $45-$60/hr |
| Bill to client | $100-$175/hr |
| **Effective markup** | **2x-3x fully-loaded cost** |

### Freelance Editor Pass-Through

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Freelance editor charges you | $60-$85/hr (DFW market) |
| Bill to client | $100-$150/hr |
| **Markup** | **25-75%** |

### Other Post Rates

| Role | Your Cost | Bill to Client |
|---|---|---|
| Colorist | $75-$150/hr or $500-$1,200/day | $125-$250/hr or $800-$1,800/day |
| Sound designer/mixer | $50-$100/hr | $85-$175/hr |

**Post as % of total budget:** 30-40% typically. On a $50K project with $20K post budget, generating $6K-$10K in markup is realistic.

## Producer Fee / Production Management Fee

### Method A: Percentage on Hard Costs (Most Common in Commercial)

Sum all hard costs (crew, equipment, locations, casting, catering, travel) and apply a single markup:

| Level | Markup |
|---|---|
| Minimum viable | 15% |
| Standard / most common | 20-22% |
| Full rate | 25-28% |
| Maximum (aggressive) | 30% |

**Example:** $40,000 hard costs x 22% = $8,800 production fee. Total bid = $48,800.

### Method B: Flat Production Fee (Common for Corporate/Branded)

| Project Size | Flat Fee |
|---|---|
| $5K-$15K project | $500-$1,500 |
| $20K-$50K project | $2,500-$7,500 |
| $75K-$150K project | $10,000-$25,000 |

**For projects under $10K:** Bake the production fee into line item rates so it's invisible to the client.
**For projects $20K+:** Break it out as a separate line item.

### Producer Day Rate (Separate Line Item)

- Working producer on a $75K project: **$800-$1,500/day** for prep + shoot + wrap days
- Director fee on commercial work: typically **7-10% of production budget** for jobs under $750K

## Agency vs. Direct Client Pricing

| Factor | Agency Work | Direct Brand Work |
|---|---|---|
| Your production fee | 20% | 25-30% |
| Creative/concept fee | Usually none (agency owns) | $3,000-$8,000 extra |
| Insurance line | 2.5-3% of production costs | Same |
| Pricing pressure | Higher (agency stacks margins) | Lower (you control) |
| Rate for equivalent work | Base | 15-30% higher |

**Why direct is higher:** You absorb creative/strategy overhead the agency normally covers, more relationship and scope-creep risk, no buffer between you and client.

## Overhead & G&A

### Lean 1-5 Person DFW Shop (No Studio)

| Category | Monthly Cost |
|---|---|
| Business insurance (GL, E&O, equipment) | $300-$600 |
| Production insurance (per project, amortized) | $150-$400 |
| Software (Adobe CC, Frame.io, PM tools) | $200-$400 |
| Vehicle (truck/SUV for gear) | $400-$800 |
| Accounting/bookkeeping | $200-$500 |
| Marketing (website, ads, reel updates) | $300-$800 |
| Storage (cloud + physical) | $100-$250 |
| Phone/internet | $150-$250 |
| Misc (office, shipping, entertainment) | $200-$500 |
| **Total** | **$2,000-$4,500/month** |

At $3,000/month = **$36,000/year** in fixed overhead.
- On $500K revenue: **7.2% overhead rate**
- On $250K revenue: **14.4% overhead rate**

**1-5 person shop overhead as % of revenue: 10-20%**

### If You Add Studio/Office Space (DFW)

- Studio lease in Dallas suburbs: $2,500-$6,000/month
- Adds ~6-15% to overhead rate depending on revenue volume

## Target Profit Margins

### By Company Type

| Company Type | Gross Margin | Net Margin |
|---|---|---|
| Owner-operated (1-2 person, you do DP + edit) | 45-65% | 25-40% |
| Small company (3-6 people) | 35-50% | 15-25% |
| Mid-size agency-level (10-20 people) | 40-55% | 12-20% |

**Healthy benchmark:** 20% net margin for a well-run small corporate/commercial shop. 25%+ is excellent.

### By Project Size

| Project Range | Target Gross Margin |
|---|---|
| $5K-$15K (corporate, social) | 25-40% |
| $20K-$50K (commercial, brand) | 30-45% |
| $75K-$150K (campaign, broadcast) | 35-50% |

| Lever | Minimum | Standard | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|
| Crew markup on freelancers | 20% | 25% | 30%+ |
| Sub-rental markup | 10% | 15% | 20% |
| Owned equipment rate | 40% of rental | Market rental rate | Market + demand premium |

| Net company margin | 10% | 18-20% | 25%+ |

# 3. PROPOSAL FRAMEWORKS & BUDGET TEMPLATES

## AICP Bid Format

The Association of Independent Commercial Producers standard. 311 line items, Sections A through X. Used for TV commercials and high-end digital/social through ad agencies.

**Use when:** Agency bids, national/regional commercials, projects $50K+, competitive bids
**Skip when:** Corporate video, documentary, social content, direct-to-client, projects under $25K

### AICP Sections (Abbreviated)

| Section | Coverage |
|---|---|
| A | Pre-Production & Wrap Labor |
| B | Shooting Crew Labor |
| C | Pre-Production Expenses |
| D | Location & Travel |
| E | Props, Wardrobe & Animals |
| F | Studio & Stage Expenses |
| G-H | Art Department Labor & Expenses |
| I | Equipment Costs |
| K | Miscellaneous Production |
| L | Director/Creative Fees |
| M-N | Talent Labor & Expenses |
| O | Editorial |
| P | Transfers & Dubs |
| Q | Visual Effects |
| R | Audio Post |
| S | Color Grading |
| T | Title & Graphics |
| U | Stock Footage/Music |
| V-W | Deliverables & Social/Digital |

Production fee (15-25%) applied on top of all line items. Insurance: 2.5-3% of production costs as separate line item.

## Standard Budget Phase Split

| Phase | % of Total Budget |
|---|---|
| Pre-Production | 20-25% |
| Production (Shoot Days) | 50-60% |
| Post-Production | 20-25% |
| Contingency | 10% (built in or added) |

## Good / Better / Best Tiered Proposals

The most persuasive format at mid-market. 60-70% of clients choose the middle tier.

**Price ratios:** Good at 1.0x, Better at 1.5-1.8x, Best at 2.5-3.0x.

### Example: Corporate Brand Video (Texas Market)

**Good — Essentials ($4,500-$7,500)**
- 1 shoot day, 1 DP (no separate director)
- Run-and-gun, available light + 1-2 LED panels
- No art direction or styling
- 1 location (client's facility), client spokesperson only
- 2 deliverables (1:30 hero + 0:30 social cut)
- 2 revision rounds, basic color, stock music
- Turnaround: 10-14 business days

**Better — Standard ($12,000-$18,000)**
- 1-2 shoot days, dedicated director + DP
- 1st AC, Gaffer, PA
- Professional lighting package
- Basic art direction, 2 locations
- Up to 1 non-union principal (client provides)
- 3 deliverables (hero + 2 social cuts)
- Motion graphics (branded lower thirds, animated end card)
- 2 revision rounds, professional color grade, sound design + music
- Turnaround: 14-21 business days

**Best — Premium ($28,000-$45,000)**
- 2-3 shoot days, full crew (12-14 people)
- Full lighting/grip, production designer
- 2 locations (may include location fee + permit)
- Professional talent (1-2 non-union, cast and auditioned)
- Drone, teleprompter
- 5 deliverables (hero + broadcast + social cuts)
- Full motion graphics, custom/premium music
- Full color grade (DaVinci), professional audio post
- Turnaround: 21-28 business days

### What Changes Between Tiers

| Element | Good | Better | Best |
|---|---|---|---|
| Crew size | 1-2 | 4-6 | 8-14 |
| Shoot days | 1 | 1-2 | 2-3 |
| Cameras | 1 | 2 | 2-3 |
| Lighting | Practical/LED | Package | Full grip/electric |
| Talent | Client-provided | Client-provided | Cast/auditioned |
| Drone | No | Maybe | Yes |
| Motion graphics | Basic/none | Branded | Custom/animated |
| Music | Stock | Licensed | Custom or premium |
| Deliverables | 2 | 3-4 | 5+ |

## Line-Item vs. Bundled Proposals

**Line-item:** Every cost listed individually. More transparent, more vulnerable to price-shopping. Best for enterprise, healthcare, government clients who require detailed budget approval.

**Bundled:** Single price per deliverable or package tier. Less scrutiny. Better for smaller clients and creative agency relationships. Protects margin.

**Most mid-market DFW companies use a hybrid:** Show phase totals (pre, production, post) but not individual crew line items.

## Scope of Work Document Structure

1. **Project Overview** — 2-3 sentence description, client name, intended use, distribution platform
2. **Deliverables** — Explicit list with specs: "One (1) :90 hero video in H.264 1920x1080"
3. **Creative Approach** — Visual style, tone, references
4. **Project Phases** — Pre-production, Production, Post-production with dates
5. **Timeline & Milestones** — Specific dates for rough cut, feedback, picture lock, final delivery
6. **Crew & Resources** — Roles being engaged
7. **Client Responsibilities** — Location access, talent, brand assets, feedback within X business days
8. **Exclusions** — What is NOT included (critical for scope creep protection)
9. **Revision Policy** — Rounds included, what constitutes a revision vs. scope change
10. **Change Order Process** — How additional work is requested and priced
11. **Payment Schedule** — Milestone-tied amounts
12. **File Delivery Specs** — Codec, resolution, frame rate, color space, audio

### Scope Creep Protection Language

> "This SOW does not include: additional shoot days beyond those listed; talent casting, auditions, or talent fees not specified herein; location fees for any location not listed; music licensing fees exceeding $[X]; broadcast mastering or closed captioning; foreign language versions; revisions beyond [N] rounds as defined in Section [X]."

### Define "Revision" Precisely

> "A revision is defined as aesthetic adjustments to existing delivered content (color, pacing, music, text updates, minor timing changes) that do not require new footage to be captured or new asset creation. Any request requiring new footage, new design assets, or substantive script changes constitutes a change in scope and will be addressed via a Change Order."

## Change Orders

| Service | Rate |
|---|---|
| Additional edit day | $800-$1,500 |
| Additional shoot day | Full production day rate |
| Additional deliverable cut | $200-$600 per cut |
| Motion graphics (per element) | $300-$1,000 |
| Additional revision round | $300-$1,000 flat or $75-$150/hr |
| Rush fee (under 48-hour turnaround) | 25-50% surcharge |
| Reshoots due to client error | Full production day rate |

## Revision Policy

- **Industry standard:** 2 rounds included (some offer 3)
- **One round =** all changes from all stakeholders compiled into a single document, submitted once
- **Additional rounds:** $300-$1,000/round or $75-$150/hour
- **Not a revision (scope change):** reshooting footage, adding footage not captured, rewriting script after picture lock, adding deliverable formats not in SOW

# 4. RETAINER & RECURRING REVENUE MODELS

## Monthly Retainer Tiers

| Tier | Monthly Rate | Includes |
|---|---|---|
| Entry | $2,000-$4,000 | 1 shoot day, 2-4 edits, 2 revision rounds, basic strategy |
| Standard | $5,000-$8,000 | 2 shoot days, 4-8 deliverables, strategy calls, motion graphics |
| Growth | $8,000-$15,000 | 3-4 shoot days, 8-16 deliverables, multi-format repurposing, reporting |
| Enterprise | $15,000-$30,000+ | Dedicated team, multiple locations, full content calendar, creative direction |

## Social Media Content Packages

### Per-Video Market Rates (A La Carte)

| Format | Rate |
|---|---|
| Instagram Reel / TikTok (15-60 sec) | $1,000-$3,000 |
| YouTube video (2-5 min, talking head + b-roll) | $3,000-$8,000 |
| LinkedIn thought leadership (60-90 sec) | $1,500-$3,500 |
| Brand narrative / hero (1-3 min) | $5,000-$15,000+ |

### Package Tiers

**$3K-$5K/month (Entry)**
- 1 shoot day, 4 short-form videos, captions + basic overlays
- 1 strategy call, client handles scheduling

**$5K-$8K/month (Standard)**
- 1-2 shoot days, 6-8 videos across 2 platforms
- Motion graphics, thumbnails, content calendar, 2 revision rounds

**$10K-$15K/month (Growth)**
- 2-3 shoot days, 10-16 deliverables
- Platform-native reformatting, trend research, monthly strategy debrief

**$15K-$25K/month (Full-Service)**
- 3-4+ shoot days, 20+ deliverables
- Multi-platform publishing, paid social creative testing, quarterly brand audit

## Contract Terms

- **Minimum initial commitment:** 90 days / 3 months (industry standard)
- **Default retainer period:** 12 months
- **After initial term:** Month-to-month with 30-day cancellation notice
- **Annual commitment discount:** 10-20% off monthly rate
- **Annual contract (monthly billing) discount:** 5-15% vs. month-to-month
- **Retainer vs. per-project discount:** 15-25% savings for client

## Content-as-a-Service (CaaS)

The positioning shift from vendor to embedded content partner. Instead of "we make videos," it's "we own your content pipeline."

| Model | Monthly Rate |
|---|---|
| Entry CaaS (editing only, client provides footage) | $1,000-$2,500 |
| CaaS with production | $4,000-$15,000 |
| Full embedded CaaS (strategy + production + reporting) | $10,000-$30,000+ |

**Key language:** Don't call it "retainer." Call it "a dedicated content partnership" or "your embedded content team."

## Fractional Creative Director Model

Sell creative leadership as a retainer add-on. Client gets an outsourced head of content without a $150K-$250K full-time hire.

| Level | Hours/Week | Monthly Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Entry fractional CD | 2-4 hrs | $2,000-$4,500 |
| Mid-level fractional CD | 4-8 hrs | $4,500-$8,000 |
| Senior fractional CD / ECD | Strategic oversight | $8,000-$15,000 |
| Full-time equivalent | 20+ hrs | $10,000-$20,000 |

**Bundled example:** $12,000/month = $5,000 fractional CD layer + $7,000 production (2 shoot days, 8 deliverables)

## Hybrid: Retainer Base + Project Overage

The most practical model for a lean studio:

- **Base retainer:** Covers monthly baseline (1 shoot day, 4 videos, strategy). Guaranteed income floor.
- **Project work:** Anything outside retainer scope quoted separately at standard or slightly discounted rates.

**Example annual client value:**
- Retainer: $5,000/month x 12 = $60,000
- Product launch (separate SOW): $18,000
- Trade show recap: $4,500
- **Total: $82,500/year**

## Revenue Stabilization: The Retainer Stack

**Goal:** Build retainer stack that covers fixed monthly overhead. All project work becomes margin.

**Example stack for a 5-person Texas studio:**

| Client | Monthly |
|---|---|
| 1 enterprise retainer | $12,000 |
| 2 mid-market retainers | $5,000 each ($10,000) |
| 1 growth retainer | $3,500 |
| **Total guaranteed MRR** | **$25,500** |

Annual guaranteed baseline: $306,000. Project work on top provides growth.

**Target:** Retainers cover 60-70% of total revenue. Project work handles 30-40%.

## Upsell Path: One-Off to Enterprise

| Stage | Revenue | Key Move |
|---|---|---|
| 1. One-off project | $5K-$25K | Over-deliver. Ask "What's next on your content roadmap?" |
| 2. Pilot retainer (90 days) | $3K-$5K/month | Make first 90 days exceptional. Document results. |
| 3. Full retainer (12 months) | $5K-$15K/month | Present annual proposal, upgrade scope, add fractional CD |
| 4. Embedded partner | $15K-$30K+/month | Own their content strategy. Tie work to business outcomes. |

**Conversion rate:** Companies using structured pitch process convert ~42% of one-off clients into retainer agreements.

**Best time to pitch retainer:** At end of first project, during delivery. Client is at peak satisfaction.

## How DFW Shops Handle Proposals by Tier

**Budget:** Phone/email quote, same-day. One-page PDF with total price. Emphasis on speed.

**Mid-market:** Discovery call, written proposal with narrative scope + line-item budget (pre/production/post). 50% upfront, 50% on delivery.

**Premium:** No rate discussion until scoping is complete. Chemistry call, detailed brief, proposal with creative approach + team credentials + timeline + budget range. Phases, not line items.

**Agency-facing:** Formal RFP response. Budget pre-established by client. Proposal competes on creative approach and team.

# 7. LICENSING & USAGE RIGHTS

## The Three Variables

Every usage fee is determined by:
1. **Territory:** Local, regional, national, worldwide
2. **Medium/Platform:** Social, digital/web, broadcast TV, OOH, cinema
3. **Duration:** 30 days, 6 months, 1 year, 3 years, perpetual

## Territory Multipliers (Local = 1x)

| Territory | Multiplier |
|---|---|
| Local (DFW only) | 1x (no uplift) |
| Regional (Texas + surrounding) | 1.5x-2x |
| National | 3x-4x |
| Worldwide | 5x-8x |

| Platform | Rate |

# 8. PAYMENT TERMS & KILL FEES

## Deposit Structures

| Structure | When to Use |
|---|---|
| **50/50** | Projects under $25K. 50% at signing, 50% at delivery. |
| **30/40/30** | Projects $25K-$75K. 30% signing, 40% at shoot completion, 30% at delivery. |
| **33/33/33** | Larger projects $50K+. Kickoff, offline edit, final handoff. |
| **100% upfront** | Short-turnaround or new clients with no relationship |

## Net Terms

- Standard: **Net 30** (market default)
- Faster cash flow: **Net 15** for final-delivery invoices
- Enterprise clients (Toyota, AA, AT&T in DFW): expect 45-60 day AP cycles

## Rush Fees

| Timeline | Surcharge |
|---|---|
| Compressed but manageable (1-2 weeks vs. 3-4) | 25% |
| Very short (under 5-7 business days) | 50% |
| Same-day / next-day | 100% (double rate) |
| Booking with less than 72 hours notice | 50% of shoot day budget |

## Weekend / Holiday Rates

| Day | Multiplier |
|---|---|
| Saturday | 1.25x-1.5x |
| Sunday / Holiday | 1.5x-2x |
| Alternative: flat premium | $200-$500/day per crew member |

## Overtime

- **Hours 10-12:** 1.5x hourly rate
- **Hours 12-14:** 2x (double time)
- **Beyond 14-16:** Double time continues
- **Non-union Texas simplified:** Flat day rate covers 10 hours, negotiate OT rate in advance ($50-$150/hr per crew member beyond 10)

## Kill Fees / Cancellation

| Timing | Fee |
|---|---|
| Before any pre-production | 10-25% of total contract |
| During pre-production | 25-50% |
| After shoot days complete | 50-75% |
| During post-production | 75-100% |
| Less than 72 hours notice | 50% of shoot day budget |
| Day-of cancellation | 100% of day's budget |

**Rescheduling (not cancellation):** 0-25% of affected day's budget if 48-72 hours notice.

# 9. CONTRACT ESSENTIALS

## MSA Key Clauses

1. **Services** — Types of services provided; specifics in SOW
2. **Payment terms** — Net 15 or Net 30. Late fee: 1.5%/month on overdue
3. **Ownership & IP** — Deliverables become client property on final payment. Raw footage, project files, proprietary tools remain production company property unless separately purchased. Raw footage license if requested: $500-$2,000 additional.
4. **License grant** — Non-exclusive, royalty-free for agreed channels only. Use beyond agreed channels requires renegotiation.
5. **Representations & warranties** — You have rights to create work. Client's materials don't infringe. Client responsible for their own talent clearances.
6. **Indemnification** — Mutual. Each party covers third-party claims from their own breach. Client indemnifies for client-provided materials.
7. **Insurance requirements:**
   - General Liability: $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate (bump to $5M for public locations)
   - Workers' Comp: required when crew is hired
   - Equipment/Inland Marine: covers owned/rented gear
   - Professional Liability (E&O): $1M per occurrence / $3M aggregate
   - Client often requires additional insured on GL policy
8. **Kill fees / cancellation** — Sliding scale (see above)
9. **Force majeure** — Weather, government orders, strikes. Rescheduling fees passed through at cost.
10. **Confidentiality / NDA** — Mutual. Portfolio/showreel usage unless client-specific NDA (may carry additional fee).
11. **Limitation of liability** — Total liability capped at amount paid under applicable SOW. No consequential damages.
12. **Dispute resolution** — Jurisdiction (Texas, Collin County). Mediation before arbitration or litigation.

## Project Minimums

| Company Type | Minimum Engagement |
|---|---|
| Freelance videographer | No floor ($500-$1,500) |
| Small production company (1-3 people) | $2,500-$5,000 |
| Mid-market (3-8 staff) | $7,500-$15,000 |
| Full-service agency with dedicated team | $20,000-$25,000 |

**DFW mid-tier common floor:** $7,500-$10,000. Premium/agency-facing: $15,000+.

# 10. GLM POSITIONING STRATEGY

## Where GLM Fits

Based on portfolio (SpaceX, ESPN/Cowboys, Ford, Troy Aikman, USPA, Tige Boats, Coca-Cola, Chevrolet) and Joey's DP rate ($1,200 floor / $2,500 target), GLM sits at the **upper mid-market to emerging premium** tier in DFW.

## Recommended Positioning

- **Set a project minimum of $7,500-$10,000.** Signals professional tier without cutting off the mid-market.
- **Use Good/Better/Best tiered proposals** for direct-to-brand work.

## Key Differentiators vs. DFW Competition

1. **Owner is the DP** — Most production companies outsource the camera department. Joey IS the camera department. Zero markup on the most expensive crew position.
2. **Equipment ownership** — Own the camera package, charge market rental rates. Pure profit on every shoot.
3. **National-caliber portfolio** — SpaceX, ESPN, Ford, Coca-Cola in a market where most mid-tier shops top out at regional brands.
4. **Lean overhead** — 1-5 person core + freelance crew means lower breakeven, higher margins, faster decisions.
