## PLATFORM 1: ProductionHUB
**URL:** https://www.productionhub.com  
**Priority Rating: HIGH**

### What It Is
The largest global network dedicated specifically to film, video, TV, live event, and post-production professionals. Founded 1999 — 27 years in the industry. One of the oldest and most established platforms of its kind. Not a general freelancer marketplace; this is purpose-built for production.

### Homepage Value Props (Direct from Site)
- "Your Film & Video Production Partner — We make it easy to find and hire qualified film, video and live event professionals. Anywhere you need them."
- 700,000+ users
- 86%+ hiring success rate
- 6 million+ searches/year
- 150,000+ verified profiles
- "Serving the Film & Video Production Industry Since 1999"

### Pricing for Pros
- **Free basic listing** available (limited visibility)
- **Paid featured listing:** Historically ranges ~$29–79/month depending on tier (pricing pages require JS login to fully render; confirmed as subscription-based from platform structure)
- Clients can also post lead requests, and pros respond ("crew leads" model)
- "Vendor leads" paid access for inbound project opportunities
- No per-lead fee model; subscription unlocks inbound lead access

### How Clients Hire / How Pros Get Found
- Clients search by location (300+ searchable categories), keyword, or role
- Clients post "crew call" RFPs and platform matches to qualified pros
- Pros get profile visibility in search results + can respond to posted leads
- Full-service crewing option also available (managed placement)

### Geographic Scope
**Global**, with strong USA concentration. The Dallas/North Texas market is explicitly referenced in homepage testimonials (Luisa Mendoza, DP Dallas TX).

### Commercial Video Relevance
**Very high.** This is not a wedding/events platform. Categories include camera operators, DPs, producers, gaffers, directors — specifically for commercial, TV, corporate, and documentary work. Active commercial production leads visible in crawl (commercial photographers, field producers, etc.).

### Platform Maturity / Reputation
Top-tier. 27 years old, active blog with industry coverage (NAB Show, Sports Video Group integrations). Testimonials from long-term subscribers (10+ years). Multiple pros cite it as their #1 highest-ROI advertising spend. Verified profile system adds credibility.

### Red Flags
- Pricing pages require JavaScript/login to see exact current rates — confirm actual pricing after signup
- Older UI compared to newer platforms
- Some listings include entry-level crew which dilutes search results

### Joey Fit Assessment
**Strong match.** Joey's DP/commercial production background fits the platform's core audience. North Texas DPs are actively listed. The platform serves exactly the client type who hires a DP with 20+ years and a gear truck — not budget shoppers. Worth getting a listed/featured profile here immediately.

**Priority Rating: MEDIUM**

One of the oldest film and TV crew/jobs databases globally. Founded 1996 in the UK. Operates as Mandy.com with US, UK, Australian, and international job boards. Part of the Mandy Network which also includes The Stage (theatre) and similar publications.

### Homepage Value Props

- Film and TV jobs board with global crew database
- Professionals list their credits, CV, and availability
- Productions post casting calls and crew calls
- Focus: scripted TV, film, commercials, music videos

### Pricing for Pros
- **Free basic listing** (limited, no response capability)
- **Standard subscription:** Approximately $24–$30/month or ~$180–$200/year (varies by country)
- Paid membership unlocks ability to respond to jobs and contact productions
- No per-lead fee model

### How Clients Hire / How Pros Get Found
- Productions search the talent/crew database by role and location
- Productions post job listings; subscribed pros can apply

**Global**, but strongest in UK and Australia. US market is secondary to UK. For North Texas, reach will be more limited than ProductionHUB.

### Commercial Video Relevance
**Moderate.** Strong for scripted film and TV work. Commercial production is covered but the platform skews more toward narrative/scripted work and UK productions. US commercial advertisers less likely to be searching here vs. ProductionHUB.

Well-established (30 years). Respected in UK film/TV community. Less dominant in the US market compared to its UK standing. Has not significantly evolved its UX in recent years — site can feel dated.

### Red Flags
- Cloudflare blocks non-browser traffic (bot-protection heavy), indicating active security but also that their traffic may be heavily protected/filtered
- UK-centric; US commercial production clients are less active here

## PLATFORM 3: Bark.com
**URL:** https://www.bark.com  
**Priority Rating: LOW**

### What It Is
UK-founded (2014) lead generation marketplace for local services. Operates across many service categories from accounting and legal to health and wellness, events, and general photography/video. Now active in US market. Not a production-industry-specific platform.

### Homepage Value Props (From Live Crawl)
- "Discover Local and National US Professionals Now"
- Broad service categories: Business, Events & Entertainers, Health & Wellness, House & Home, Lessons & Training
- Video production falls under general photography/videography category
- Designed for local service matching — customer describes need, relevant pros are notified

### Pricing for Pros
**Credit-based model (not subscription):**
- Pros buy "credits" in bundles (approx. $1.50–$3.00 per credit at market rates)
- Each lead "costs" 3–10+ credits to unlock contact info, depending on job size/type
- Effective cost per lead: approximately $5–$30+ per lead attempt
- No subscription required, but pros must keep credits funded
- Free to create a profile; costs only when responding to leads

### How Clients Hire / How Pros Get Found
- Client fills out a request form describing their project
- Bark alerts up to 5 matching local pros
- Pros pay credits to access the lead and contact the client
- Platform has no exclusivity — multiple pros compete for same lead
- Client then selects based on quotes and reviews

### Geographic Scope
**USA and UK**, plus other markets. Very local/proximity-based matching.

### Commercial Video Relevance
**Low.** Bark.com does not have a commercial film/advertising production category. Videographers show up under general photography. The client base is heavily skewed toward weddings, events, social media content, and general corporate B-roll — not $50K+ commercial productions. Clients sourcing a crew for a Super Bowl-level project are not on Bark.

### Platform Maturity / Reputation
Growing platform with mixed reviews. Significant complaints from pros about lead quality — many leads are tire-kickers or have minimal budgets. The credit model means pros spend money without guaranteed return. Consumer review sites show recurring frustrations from service providers about low close rates on purchased leads.

### Red Flags
- Credit model = you pay for leads even if they ghost, have no budget, or never respond
- Extremely broad marketplace — no production-industry vetting
- Lead quality is inconsistent and often budget-sensitive (not Joey's target client)
- Competes on volume and price, not expertise and credits
- Not a production industry platform — Joey would be listed next to wedding photographers

### Joey Fit Assessment
**Not a fit.** Bark.com serves a completely different client tier and project type than Joey's commercial work. The credit-based model with low-budget leads would be a time and money drain. Do not add to CRM as a prioritized channel.

## PLATFORM 4: Thumbtack
**URL:** https://www.thumbtack.com  
**Priority Rating: LOW**

### What It Is
US-only local services marketplace founded in 2009. Primarily focused on home improvement, home repair, and related services (cleaning, handymen, landscaping, plumbing, HVAC, etc.). Has a creative services category but it is a secondary feature. Backed by significant venture capital and heavily consumer-facing.

### Homepage Value Props (From Live Crawl)
- "Home improvement, made easy."
- "Trusted by 4.5M+ people"
- "4.9/5 with over 300k reviews on the App Store"
- "Pros for every project in Rockwall, Texas" (location-detected)
- Main categories: Cleaners, Handymen, Landscapers, Movers, Plumbers, Electricians, Painters, HVAC
- Guarantees up to $2,500 if job isn't done as agreed

### Pricing for Pros
**Pay-per-contact model:**
- Free to create a profile
- Pros pay a fee when a customer contacts them (not when they contact customers)
- Lead prices vary by service type and location — creative/video services typically $15–60+ per lead contact
- Pros can set a weekly budget cap
- No monthly subscription required

### How Clients Hire / How Pros Get Found
- Customer describes project in natural language
- Platform matches to local pros based on category and location
- Customers can view profiles, reviews, and contact multiple pros
- Pros who "instant match" appear higher in results

### Geographic Scope
**USA-only.** "Don't worry about finding a pro — we cover every county in the U.S."

### Commercial Video Relevance
**Very low.** Thumbtack is fundamentally a home services platform that happens to have a videographer category. The clientele is overwhelmingly consumers looking for wedding videographers, event coverage, and social media content creators — not brand managers searching for a commercial DP with a gear truck. Budget expectations are completely misaligned with Joey's positioning.

### Platform Maturity / Reputation
Well-established, well-funded consumer platform. Strong reputation in home services. Not respected in professional commercial production circles. Other professionals in creative services have noted poor lead quality and high lead costs relative to conversion.

### Red Flags
- Home services platform first; creative services are an afterthought
- Lead model means paying for contacts that are overwhelmingly not commercial production clients
- Competing against wedding videographers at consumer price points — damaging to premium positioning
- No production-industry context or vetting
- Appearing here alongside house cleaners and handymen is a brand positioning risk for high-end commercial work

### Joey Fit Assessment
**Not a fit.** Thumbtack clients expect consumer pricing. A commercial DP with Joey's credits, gear, and experience would be invisible or off-brand here. Not worth the time or money.

It was acquired and became part of Glimmer Technologies, Inc. The platform now operates as Glimmer.io with an expanded scope but the same core mission: connecting brands and media companies with professional video creators and production crews globally.

### Pricing for Pros
- **Application/approval required** — creators are vetted before listing
- Free to apply as a creator; platform takes a commission on booked work (typically 15–20% per industry norms for managed platforms like this)
- Enterprise clients pay platform fees separately
- No subscription cost for creators — earn-when-you-work model

### How Clients Hire / How Pros Get Found
- Brands and enterprise clients use Glimmer to discover vetted creators by specialty, location, and past work
- "Glimmer Select" is a managed/white-glove service connecting clients to pre-vetted production teams

### Geographic Scope
**Global** — 192 countries for Glimmer Select. Active in US market. The platform serves US-based brands (Meta, Starbucks) which means US-based DPs and directors are in demand.

### Commercial Video Relevance
**High.** This is a video-first platform serving major brands. Unlike the Storyhunter era which focused more on news/documentary content, Glimmer now focuses on branded content, social campaigns, and commercial work. Evolving. Storyhunter was well-regarded in its era. Glimmer is newer as a rebrand but has significant client backing. The transition means some instability, but the platform is actively operating and booking creators. Worth monitoring as the platform matures.

### Red Flags

- Commission-based model means giving up 15–20% per project booked through platform
- Skews toward social media / "content creator" framing which is below Joey's positioning tier
- Approval process — not guaranteed acceptance

Apply with full commercial credits (Super Bowl, U.S. Polo Assn., SpaceX Starlink) and position as a commercial production expert, not a content creator. The vetted marketplace means less race-to-the-bottom competition. Monitor for a few months post-application to assess lead volume.

## PLATFORM 6: StaffMeUp
**URL:** https://www.staffmeup.com  
**Priority Rating: MEDIUM**

### What It Is
TV, film, and digital production job board founded in 2003. One of the most established platforms specifically for entertainment industry crew hiring. Markets itself as "Home to the Best Jobs in Media and Entertainment." Serves both freelancers seeking gigs and productions hiring crew.

### Homepage Value Props (From Live Crawl)
- "The Most Trusted Network for Production Jobs in TV, Film and Digital"
- 6,251+ active jobs right now
- 489,000+ production staff & crew in database
- 145,930+ jobs posted since 2003
- Testimonials from Camera Operators, Hair/Makeup, Production Assistants — all citing consistent hires
- "Staff Me Up has always been a go-to tool for me as a freelancer." — multiple pro testimonials

### Pricing for Pros (Confirmed from Live Pricing Page)
- **Basic:** Free — limited to 5 job applications/month
- **Premium Monthly:** $19.99/month — unlimited applications, promoted applications, job analytics, featured resume, search all freelancers
- **Premium Annual:** $12.50/month (billed as $149.99/year) — same benefits as monthly, best deal
- Site was advertising $50–$65 off annual with promo codes

### How Clients Hire / How Pros Get Found
- Productions post jobs; pros apply
- Premium pros get promoted applications (stand out vs. basic)
- Hiring managers browse the freelancer database
- Enterprise tier available for large production companies
- Job alerts by text and email for keyword-matched positions

### Geographic Scope
**USA primarily**, with some international entertainment industry presence. Strong in Hollywood, New York, and major production markets. Less saturated in regional markets like Dallas/North Texas (which can be an advantage).

### Commercial Video Relevance
**Moderate.** StaffMeUp skews heavily toward scripted TV, reality TV, streaming, and film production. Commercial advertising production is present but secondary. The platform's sweet spot is episodic television crew — not brand commercial production. That said, commercial DP and gaffer gigs do appear.

### Platform Maturity / Reputation
Very established — 23 years. Trusted by the industry. Active and maintained platform with a mobile app. Not flashy but functional and well-regarded in crew hiring circles. Clean testimonials from working pros.

### Red Flags
- Primary focus is scripted TV/film crew, not commercial advertising production
- Dallas/North Texas commercial market may have limited active postings
- $149.99/year is low cost, but ROI depends on commercial job volume in the region
- Entry-level crew (PAs, camera assistants) dominate the user base — Joey is competing for attention in a DP-specific tier

### Joey Fit Assessment
**Worth the $150/year investment** to maintain a Premium Annual profile and monitor for DP/director/gaffer opportunities. Low cost, established platform, verified industry credibility. Set job alerts for Dallas/Texas DP and gaffer roles and check weekly. Not a primary lead source but worth the small spend.

## PLATFORM 7: ProductionBeast
**URL:** https://www.productionbeast.com  
**Priority Rating: MEDIUM-LOW**

### What It Is
A video-industry job board owned by StudioBinder (the production management software company). Positioned as "A Mighty Job Board for Video Professionals." Launched approximately 2017. Lightweight platform — less features than ProductionHUB or StaffMeUp but completely free for freelancers.

### Homepage Value Props (From Live Crawl)
- "Where productions discover and hire the best creative professionals."
- Job alerts, profile creation, and direct applications — all free for pros
- For employers: free job posting, database search, candidate management tools
- For vendors: featured placement, lead analytics, team collaboration tools (paid tier for vendors)
- "I got over 58 resumes in two days, more than I expected." — Senior Director, Marketing Operations (client testimonial)
- Trusted by recognizable companies (mentioned in crawl)

### Pricing for Pros
**Free for freelancers.** No subscription cost to create a profile, apply to jobs, or receive alerts. Vendor featured listings have a paid option (pricing not disclosed without login). No per-application fee.

### How Clients Hire / How Pros Get Found
- Productions post jobs (free); pros apply via profile
- Productions can also search the freelancer database directly
- Vendor directory (for companies offering services) has paid placement options
- Job alerts sent to matching pro profiles by email

### Geographic Scope
**USA-focused**, global in scope. No hard geographic restrictions.

### Commercial Video Relevance
**Moderate.** ProductionBeast is video-industry specific (not general services). Categories include camera operators, DPs, directors, editors, producers. Commercial production is covered. However, the platform is lighter-traffic than ProductionHUB or StaffMeUp — owned by a software company as a secondary product, not a standalone industry leader.

### Platform Maturity / Reputation
Functional but not industry-dominant. Blog articles are well-written (StudioBinder quality). Active enough to have job listings but less community-driven than the established players. Given it's a StudioBinder product, it benefits from that brand's credibility in the production software space.

### Red Flags
- Secondary product of a software company — not the primary business focus
- Lower traffic and job volume than ProductionHUB or StaffMeUp
- Vendor paid features are unclear in cost/benefit
- Less established network of clients actively searching for high-end commercial DP talent
- Copyright notice on site is 2017 — limited updates visible

### Joey Fit Assessment
**Free, so worth a profile.** Create a complete profile, set job alerts for Dallas DP/gaffer, and check monthly. Don't spend money on vendor placement until there's demonstrated inbound activity. Low time investment for whatever ROI it might generate. Not a priority but also not a cost.
