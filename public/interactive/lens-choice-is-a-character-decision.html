02 — Reference

Focal Length Psychology

These are not rules. They are tendencies built from the physics of optics and the accumulated grammar of a century of cinema. Hover to explore each register.

12–24mm
Extreme Wide — Exposure
Distortion at close range. Character cannot hide. Environment presses in from every edge. Used at proximity: vulnerability, chaos, survival.
The Revenant, The Shining, Children of Men
24–35mm
Wide — Presence
Environmental context remains visible. Character exists within a world. Favored by Deakins for faces: intimacy without isolation.
No Country for Old Men, Sicario, Fargo
35–50mm
Normal — Invisible
Closest to human field of view. No optical argument. The lens disappears. Used when the scene carries itself without formal statement from the glass.
The Godfather (Willis, family sequences), Moonlight
75–100mm
Short Tele — Watch
Conversational distance without intrusion. The character forgets the camera exists. Background begins to compress. Space begins to close.
Boogie Nights, There Will Be Blood, Phantom Thread
135–200mm
Telephoto — Fate
Compression. Entrapment. Background stacks against the subject. Character cannot escape the world closing behind them. The lens of surveillance and inevitability.
No Country for Old Men (Chigurh scenes), Sicario border crossing
03 — Format

Anamorphic vs. Spherical: A Decision About Scale

The choice of format is a choice about how much world you want around your character. Anamorphic gives the world space. Spherical gives the character space.

1.78:1
TV / Streaming

The character fills more of the vertical frame. Less world on either side. This format foregrounds the person. Used pervasively in streaming and television drama, where the story lives inside the character. Character looms. World recedes.

True Detective S1 The Last of Us Succession Breaking Bad
1.85:1
Spherical

A slight widening over 1.78. The standard theatrical spherical ratio. Character and world share the frame more evenly. Intimate drama that still wants the audience to feel the contained world around the subject. Widely used for character-centered feature films.

The Godfather Moonlight Marriage Story Her
2.39:1
Anamorphic

The character is surrounded. World extends in both directions beyond any other format. The figure is small within a vast horizontal field. This is the language of myth, of landscape, of characters who are figures within something larger than themselves. Anamorphic does not just capture the world. It insists on it.

05 — Field Notes

The Scout Decision

Roger Deakins walks every location before he shoots it. He knows what lens lives in each room before the camera package arrives. That is why his images look like decisions, not accidents.

The lens choice made on the tech scout is intention. The lens choice made on the day, under time pressure, is compromise. The difference between those two is the difference between a DP who lit the scene and a DP who designed the image.

Walk the location. Ask: what does the character feel here, and which focal length tells the audience that truth? The answer is the lens you pull from the kit.
