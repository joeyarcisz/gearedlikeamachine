02   The Three Categories

Every Source Falls Into One

Motivation exists on a spectrum from fully literal to fully abstract. The category determines how much explanatory work the scene must do before you earn the light.

Category 01

The Practical

Source visible in frame. Cause and effect are simultaneous.

The highest form of motivation. The audience sees the lamp, the window, the candle. They see the light that follows. No explanation required. The scene justifies itself. Kubrick shot all of Barry Lyndon's candlelit scenes by candlelight alone. The candles in frame were the only source. No supplementation. Complete motivation.

Category 02

Implied Source

No practical visible. But geography makes it believable.

The window is off-frame. The streetlight is outside. The audience infers the source because the world has established it. Quality, direction, and color temperature must be consistent. Deakins in Blade Runner 2049: holographic advertisements and industrial fixtures are rarely in frame. The light always traces to the established world geography.

Category 03

Atmospheric Source

Light traces to emotional state, not physical object.

The most advanced and most dangerous. Light justified by the internal logic of the scene's psychological state. Requires a coherent visual world built first, or it reads as careless. Storaro lit Brando in Apocalypse Now from a hard shaft with no traceable practical. He earned the abstraction by establishing the compound's visual chaos first.
